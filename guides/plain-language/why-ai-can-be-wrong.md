# Why AI Can Be Wrong

AI can help you search, compare, and summarize. It can also sound confident when it is wrong.

## Common Failure Modes

### It Fills Gaps

AI is trained to produce complete answers. Genealogy often has incomplete evidence. If a parent is unknown, the right answer may be “unknown.”

### It Confuses Same-Name People

Many records contain people with the same name, similar birth year, and nearby locations. AI may merge them too quickly.

### It Copies Weak Trees

Online family trees often copy each other. Ten matching trees may still trace back to one unsourced guess.

### It Misreads Source Quality

An index, obituary, user tree, and birth certificate are not equal. AI can cite all of them with the same confidence unless you force source grading.

### It Over-Explains DNA

DNA ethnicity labels and small segments are not exact historical facts. They should point to research questions, not final conclusions.

## Fictional Example

The AI finds an online tree saying Casey North's mother was Dana North. It also finds a census record with Casey living near a Dana North.

That may be useful. It is still not proof unless the records connect them as parent and child, or independent sources support the relationship.

## How To Protect Yourself

- Use [Verify An AI Finding](../../checklists/verify-an-ai-finding.md).
- Require sources before facts.
- Keep weak leads marked `speculative`.
- Log negative searches.
- Run [Cross-Reference Audit](../../prompts/02-cross-reference-audit.md) after expansion.
- Reject answers that make the tree bigger but not better supported.
