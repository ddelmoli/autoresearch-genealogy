# Austria

Austrian genealogical records are well-preserved, particularly for Vienna. Access requires knowledge of multiple databases across religious and civil authorities.

## Key Databases

### Matricula Online
- **URL**: https://data.matricula-online.eu/
- **Coverage**: Catholic parish records (baptism, marriage, death) for Austria, Germany, Poland, and other countries
- **Cost**: Free
- **Searchable**: Browse-only (navigate by diocese, parish, record type, date range)
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only. Navigation requires knowing the specific parish.

### ANNO (Austrian Newspapers Online)
- **URL**: https://anno.onb.ac.at/
- **Coverage**: Historical Austrian newspapers, fully digitized and OCR-searchable
- **Cost**: Free
- **Searchable**: Full-text search
- **AI accessibility**: AI-searchable for finding articles; AI-readable for article text
- **Key use**: Obituaries, death notices, business announcements, wedding announcements in papers like Neue Freie Presse

### Lehmann Vienna Directories
- **URL**: https://www.digital.wienbibliothek.at/ (navigate to Lehmann directories)
- **Coverage**: Vienna address directories, 1859 to 1942
- **Cost**: Free
- **Searchable**: OCR-searchable within individual volumes
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only (requires navigating the Wien Bibliothek viewer interface)
- **Key use**: Track a family's address and occupation year by year

### GenTeam
- **URL**: https://www.genteam.at/
- **Coverage**: Indexed Austrian records including Vienna Jewish records, military records, citizenship records
- **Cost**: Free (registration required)
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only (requires login)

### Friedhöfe Wien (Vienna Cemeteries)
- **URL**: https://www.friedhoefewien.at/
- **Coverage**: Vienna municipal cemetery burial database
- **Cost**: Free
- **Searchable**: Yes (by name)
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only (interactive search form)

### IKG Wien (Jewish Community Vienna)
- **URL**: Contact via email (w.eckstein@ikg-wien.at)
- **Coverage**: Vienna Jewish vital records (births, marriages, deaths) 1784 to 1938
- **Cost**: Research fees apply
- **AI accessibility**: In-person/email only
- **Key use**: Essential for researching Jewish families in Vienna

### Austrian State Archives (Österreichisches Staatsarchiv)
- **URL**: https://www.oesta.gv.at/
- **Coverage**: Military records (Kriegsarchiv), civil records, nobility records
- **Cost**: Free for on-site research; fees for reproductions
- **AI accessibility**: In-person only for most records

## Vienna-Specific Research

Vienna's multi-ethnic, multi-religious population requires searching across multiple record systems:

| Community | Records | Database |
|---|---|---|
| Catholic | Parish registers (Tauf-, Trauungs-, Sterbebücher) | Matricula Online |
| Jewish | Community vital records (IKG) | IKG Wien archive, GenTeam |
| Protestant | Parish registers | Evangelisches Zentralarchiv |
| Civil | Municipal records (post-1938) | Magistratsabteilung 35 |

### Jewish-Catholic Crossover

Between 1782 and 1914, over 18,000 Jews left Judaism in Vienna (Austrittsjuden). Many converted to Catholicism for social and professional reasons. This means:
- A family that appears Catholic in parish records may have Jewish origins
- Look for conversion records (Taufbücher / baptism books noting adult baptism)
- A "Catholic" burial in a Christian cemetery does not rule out Jewish ancestry
- Ashkenazi Jewish DNA percentages may not correspond to any documented Jewish ancestor if conversion happened generations ago

## Search Strategy

1. Determine the parish or district (Bezirk) where your ancestors lived
2. Search Matricula Online for parish records in that district
3. Search ANNO for newspaper mentions (obituaries, business listings)
4. If researching Vienna, search Lehmann directories for address history
5. If Jewish ancestry is suspected, contact IKG Wien
6. For military ancestors, request records from the Kriegsarchiv
