# Scan Your Documents

Use this for certificates, letters, postcards, photos, military papers, newspaper clippings, and inherited binders.

## Capture

- [ ] Scan flat documents at 300 DPI or better.
- [ ] Scan photos at 600 DPI when possible.
- [ ] Use a document scanning app for phone captures.
- [ ] Capture the front and back of each item.
- [ ] Keep the full image, even if only one part looks useful.

## Name And Sort

- [ ] Use descriptive filenames with date, person, and document type when known.
- [ ] Put uncertain details in the note, not the filename.
- [ ] Group files by family line, person, or document type.
- [ ] Record each batch in `Data_Inventory.md`.

## Transcribe

- [ ] Create a plain-text transcription or summary for each important item.
- [ ] Preserve original spelling.
- [ ] Mark unreadable words as `[illegible]`.
- [ ] Record who created the document, when, and why.
- [ ] Add source notes before adding facts to `Family_Tree.md`.
