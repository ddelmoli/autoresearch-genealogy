# Interview A Relative

Use this when collecting family stories from a living person.

## Before

- [ ] Ask permission to take notes or record.
- [ ] Explain how the information will be used.
- [ ] Do not promise that family stories will be treated as proven facts.
- [ ] Prepare names, photos, or places that can jog memory.
- [ ] Decide what details should stay private.

## During

- [ ] Ask open questions first.
- [ ] Capture exact words for names, nicknames, places, and stories.
- [ ] Ask how the person knows each claim.
- [ ] Ask about documents, photos, letters, and family Bibles.
- [ ] Note uncertainty instead of smoothing it over.

## After

- [ ] Label the interview as oral history in your notes.
- [ ] Add possible leads to `Open_Questions.md`.
- [ ] Do not add a new ancestor from the interview alone.
- [ ] Use [Oral History Protocol](../workflows/oral-history-protocol.md).
- [ ] Verify claims with records before upgrading evidence.
