# USA: Immigration and Naturalization Records

Immigration and naturalization records are among the most valuable sources for families with immigrant ancestors, particularly for the period after 1890.

## Passenger Lists (Ship Manifests)

### Ellis Island / Statue of Liberty Foundation
- **URL**: https://www.libertyellisfoundation.org/
- **Coverage**: New York arrivals, 1892 to 1957 (~65 million records)
- **Cost**: Free
- **Searchable**: Yes (by name, year, age, origin)
- **AI accessibility**: AI-searchable

### Castle Garden
- **URL**: https://www.castlegarden.org/
- **Coverage**: New York arrivals, 1820 to 1892 (~11 million records)
- **Cost**: Free
- **Searchable**: Yes
- **AI accessibility**: AI-searchable

### FamilySearch (Passenger Lists)
- **Coverage**: Multiple US and Canadian ports
- **Cost**: Free
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only for individual records

### What Passenger Lists Contain

**Pre-1893**: Name, age, sex, occupation, country of origin, destination. Minimal detail.

**1893 to 1906**: Added: last residence, final destination in US, whether they have a ticket, who paid for passage, whether they have been in the US before, marital status, literacy.

**Post-1906**: Added: personal description (height, complexion, hair, eyes), birthplace (city and country), name and address of nearest relative in country of origin, name and address of person joining in US, whether a polygamist, whether an anarchist, physical and mental health.

**Post-1906 manifests are extremely valuable** because they list the specific city of birth in the old country, not just the country name.

## Naturalization Records

### USCIS Genealogy Program
- **URL**: https://www.uscis.gov/records/genealogy
- **Coverage**: Naturalization certificate files, 1906 to 1956
- **Cost**: $65 per request
- **AI accessibility**: Mail/online request only

### County and Federal Court Records
- **Coverage**: Naturalization petitions were filed in county, state, or federal courts
- **Cost**: Varies (many on FamilySearch for free)
- **AI accessibility**: Varies by courthouse

### What Naturalization Records Contain

**Pre-1906 (Declaration of Intent / "First Papers")**: Name, country of birth, date of arrival, renunciation of allegiance. Minimal detail.

**Post-1906 (Petition for Naturalization)**: Name, birth date, birthplace (specific town), current address, occupation, date of arrival, port and ship name, marital status, spouse's name and birthplace, children's names and birth dates, physical description, witnesses. **This is often the single most informative document for an immigrant ancestor.**

## Border Crossings

### Canadian Border Crossings
- **URL**: Available on Ancestry.com and FamilySearch
- **Coverage**: 1895 to 1956
- **Note**: Many European immigrants entered the US via Canada. If you cannot find a ship manifest for arrival at a US port, check Canadian ports and border crossings.

### Mexican Border Crossings
- **URL**: Available on FamilySearch and Ancestry.com
- **Coverage**: 1903 to 1957

## Search Strategy

1. Estimate the arrival period (when did your ancestor first appear in US records?)
2. Search Ellis Island / Castle Garden for the appropriate era
3. Try ALL name variants (original spelling, phonetic, abbreviated, maiden name)
4. If not found by name, try searching by ship (if known from family records) + approximate date
5. Search for naturalization records in the county where your ancestor lived
6. For post-1906, the naturalization petition is usually more informative than the passenger list
7. Check Canadian border crossings if US ports yield no results

## Name Variation Tips

Passenger manifest names were often recorded by immigration officials who heard the name spoken and wrote it phonetically. Expect:
- Phonetic spellings (Nowak → Novak, Nordlie → Lilahoy)
- Truncated names (Rasmusen → Rasmussen → Rasmsen)
- Translated names (Johann → John, Wilhelmine → Minnie)
- Reversed given/family names
- Missing diacritics (Müller → Mueller → Miller)
