# USA: Colonial Records (Pre-1800)

Colonial American records are fragmentary but certain record types survive well.

## Key Sources

### FamilySearch
- **URL**: https://www.familysearch.org/
- **Coverage**: Indexed and digitized records from all 13 original colonies plus territories
- **Cost**: Free (account required)
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only for individual records; wiki pages are AI-readable

### Fold3
- **URL**: https://www.fold3.com/
- **Coverage**: Military records including Revolutionary War pensions, muster rolls, bounty land warrants
- **Cost**: Subscription (some free collections)
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only

### Archive.org and Google Books
- **Coverage**: Published county histories, genealogies, and compiled records
- **Cost**: Free
- **Note**: In-copyright digitized genealogies that aren't fully readable can often be **borrowed** through the Open Library (https://openlibrary.org) with a free account.
- **AI accessibility**: AI-searchable and AI-readable

### DAR (Daughters of the American Revolution)
- **URL**: https://www.dar.org/
- **Coverage**: Patriot Index (Revolutionary War era service records), GRS database
- **Cost**: GRS database is member-only; Patriot Index is searchable online
- **AI accessibility**: Partially AI-searchable

### USGenWeb
- **URL**: https://usgenweb.org/
- **Coverage**: Volunteer transcriptions of county records organized by state and county
- **Cost**: Free
- **AI accessibility**: AI-readable

## Lineage Societies as Research Aids

Hereditary societies vet and publish member lineages, which serve as compiled (secondary) sources — useful for orientation, but verify against primary records, since society applications sometimes perpetuate errors for decades.

- **General Society of Mayflower Descendants (GSMD)**: the "Silver Books" (*Mayflower Families Through Five Generations*) document the first five generations of each Mayflower passenger's descendants — the standard starting point for Plymouth-colony Mayflower lines.
- **DAR**: beyond the Patriot Index, the Genealogical Research System (GRS) links Revolutionary War patriots to documented descendant lineages.
- **WikiTree project / Space pages** (e.g. the Mayflower project): free, sourced indexes into many of these society lineages — a quick way to locate a published line.

## Record Types by Survival Rate

| Record Type | Survival | Value | Where to Find |
|---|---|---|---|
| Land records | Excellent | Names, dates, relationships, property descriptions | County courthouses, FamilySearch |
| Probate (wills, inventories) | Good | Family relationships, property, detailed personal info | County courthouses, FamilySearch |
| Church records | Variable | Baptism, marriage, burial dates | Published compilations, denominational archives |
| Military (Revolutionary War) | Good | Service records, pension applications (rich detail) | NARA, Fold3, FamilySearch |
| Tax lists / tithables | Good | Presence in county, approximate economic status | State archives |
| Court records | Variable | Land disputes, guardianships, name changes | County courthouses |
| Census (pre-1790) | Poor | Only scattered colonial censuses exist | State archives |

## Colonial Research Challenges

### Name Spelling
Colonial records have no standardized spelling. The same person may appear as Thomas, Tomas, Thom., or Thommas in different records. Search broadly.

### Calendar Change
Britain and its colonies switched from Julian to Gregorian calendar in 1752. Dates before September 1752 may use Old Style dating where the year began on March 25 (not January 1). A date written as "February 10, 1731" in Old Style is "February 10, 1732" in New Style.

### "Junior" and "Senior"
In colonial records, these designations indicate the older and younger man of the same name in a community, not necessarily father and son. As the older man died, "Junior" might become the unmarked name.

### Women in Records
Women rarely appear by name in colonial records. They are identified as "wife of," "widow of," or by first name only. Marriage records, church records, and probate records are the best sources for women's names.

## Search Strategy

1. Identify the colony and county where your ancestor lived
2. Search FamilySearch for indexed records from that county
3. Search Google Books and Archive.org for published county histories
4. Check USGenWeb for the county page (volunteer transcriptions)
5. For Revolutionary War era, search the DAR Patriot Index and Fold3
6. For land records, search FamilySearch for county deed books
