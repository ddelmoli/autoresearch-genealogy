# Germany

German genealogical records are fragmented across religious institutions, state archives, and municipal offices due to Germany's history as many independent states.

## Key Databases

### Archion
- **URL**: https://www.archion.de/
- **Coverage**: Protestant (Evangelical) church records across Germany
- **Cost**: Subscription (from EUR 19.80/month)
- **Searchable**: Browse-only (navigate by church, record type, date)
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only (subscription required)

### Matricula Online
- **URL**: https://data.matricula-online.eu/
- **Coverage**: Catholic parish records (Germany, Austria, Poland, and others)
- **Cost**: Free
- **Searchable**: Browse-only
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only (navigate by diocese and parish)

### FamilySearch (Germany collections)
- **URL**: https://www.familysearch.org/
- **Coverage**: Indexed vital records, church records, and civil registration for many German states
- **Cost**: Free (account required)
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only for individual records

### Ancestry.com (Germany collections)
- **URL**: https://www.ancestry.com/
- **Coverage**: Various German collections including emigration records, some indexed church records
- **Cost**: Subscription
- **AI accessibility**: Browser-only

### German Regional Archives

Each German state (Bundesland) has its own state archive:

| State | Archive | URL | Notable Collections |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bavaria | Bayerisches Hauptstaatsarchiv | https://www.gda.bayern.de/ | Church records, census, military |
| Prussia (various) | Geheimes Staatsarchiv | https://www.gsta.spk-berlin.de/ | Administrative records |
| Baden-Württemberg | Landesarchiv | https://www.landesarchiv-bw.de/ | Civil registration from 1810 |
| Hessen | Hessisches Landesarchiv | https://landesarchiv.hessen.de/ | Various |

## Record Types

### Civil Registration (Standesamt)
Started at different dates depending on the region:
- Rhineland (French influence): from 1798
- Prussia: from 1874
- Bavaria: from 1876
- All of Germany: from 1876

Records are held by local civil registry offices (Standesämter). After privacy periods expire, they transfer to state archives.

### Church Records (Kirchenbücher)
The primary genealogical source before civil registration:
- Protestant records: Archion (subscription)
- Catholic records: Matricula Online (free) or diocesan archives
- Coverage typically begins in the 1600s (earlier in some areas, later in others)

### Ortsfamilienbücher (Village Family Books)
Compiled genealogies covering every family in a village or parish, usually spanning several centuries. Similar to Norwegian bygdebøker.
- Search the OFB database: https://www.online-ofb.de/
- Free, volunteer-compiled
- AI-readable (HTML-based)

## Naming Conventions

- German surnames became fixed earlier than Scandinavian ones (generally by 1600 to 1700)
- Regional naming patterns: von (nobility), zu (place of residence), diminutive suffixes (-el, -le, -chen)
- Handwriting: records before ~1940 often use Kurrent script, which is very difficult for modern readers and OCR. Claude multimodal can help with Kurrent reading.

## Search Strategy

1. Determine the specific town/village and religious denomination
2. Search FamilySearch for indexed records from that area
3. For Catholic records, check Matricula Online
4. For Protestant records, check Archion (subscription)
5. Search for an Ortsfamilienbuch covering that village
6. For post-1876, contact the local Standesamt for civil records
